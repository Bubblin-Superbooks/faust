If I must share my chamber with thee,
Poodle, stop that howling, prithee! Cease to bark and bellow! Such a noisy, disturbing fellow
I'll no longer suffer near me.
One of us, dost hear me! Must leave, I fear me.
No longer guest-right I bestow;
The door is open, art free to go.
But what do I see in the creature?
Is that in the course of nature?
Is't actual fact? or Fancy's shows?
How long and broad my poodle grows! He rises mightily:
A canine form that cannot be! What a spectre I've harbored thus! He resembles a hippopotamus,
With fiery eyes, teeth terrible to see:
O, now am I sure of thee! For all of thy half-hellish brood
The Key of Solomon is good.
